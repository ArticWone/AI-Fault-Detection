## Adjustment Attempt And Result

Question being tested: whether giving the top film more slack while the seal bar
comes down would reduce the B1/B2 alarm.

Suggested first adjustment was lowering film inverter acceleration ramp from
`2000 Hz/s` to `1800 Hz/s`. Result:

- Marker `2026-04-28T12:13:42.492-05:00`, `ACCEL_RAMP_LOWER_WORSE`
- Operator reported lowering acceleration made the fault worse: `3/3` packs failed.

Interpretation: slowing acceleration likely made film/product lag or slack worse.
Reverse that change before further tuning. Put acceleration back to `2000 Hz/s`.
If testing the opposite direction, try `2200 Hz/s` in small steps, one variable at
a time, and run several packs before judging.

Follow-up field result:

- Acceleration ramp at `2200 Hz/s` made the behavior slightly better, according
  to technician/operator feedback, but it did not resolve the fault.
- Because B2 is the slave of the tied B1/B2 pair, a B2 alarm may be reporting the
  paired zone arriving out of time rather than a bad B2 sensor by itself.
- This supports the direction that higher acceleration helped film/product
  response, but the remaining fault is probably still a timing/alignment/window
  issue around the B1/B2 pair.

Additional technician-guided test/inspection:

- B2 was unplugged to see if it could be bypassed. It could not be bypassed by
  unplugging.
- The B2 bracket is visibly not a perfect 90-degree bend. The sensor is closer
  to the metal block than the other side.
- This makes physical B2 slave-sensor geometry/alignment a stronger suspect
  than continuing inverter tuning alone.

## Working Interpretation

B12 is mapped and does not look like the main recurring fault source. B1/B2 are
packed in the `10168` / `10182` group and are tied to product position near the
seal-bar-bottom timing window. The current failure is most likely caused by
film/product timing or tension around the B1/B2 zone, not a simple failed B12
sensor.

The fact that lowering film acceleration made the failure worse is important.
It suggests the machine may need the top film to respond faster or hold better
through the seal-bar descent, rather than adding slack by softening acceleration.
